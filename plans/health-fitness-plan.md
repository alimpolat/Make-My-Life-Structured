## 3. Quick Wins (Start This Week)

You don't need a 90-day plan to start. You need three things this week. That's it.

### Quick Win 1: The Water Rule
**Action**: Drink 1 full glass of water (300ml) immediately when you wake up, before coffee.
**Why**: You wake up dehydrated after 6-7 hours without fluid. Water before coffee reduces the caffeine crash, improves digestion, and gives you a clearer head by 9 AM.
**How**: Put a glass of water on your nightstand tonight. Tomorrow morning, drink it before your feet touch the floor.

### Quick Win 2: The 10-Minute Walk
**Action**: Walk for 10 minutes after dinner. Every day. No exceptions.
**Why**: A post-dinner walk lowers blood sugar by up to 30%, aids digestion, reduces evening cortisol, and gives your brain a reset before the late-work shift. This is the single highest-ROI health habit you can start.
**How**: After you finish dinner, put on shoes and walk around the block. Take the 4-year-old if it helps. Don't make it a workout -- just walk.

### Quick Win 3: The Phone Curfew
**Action**: Phone goes on the charger in the kitchen at 10:00 PM. Not in the bedroom.
**Why**: The blue light, the doomscrolling, the "just one more video" -- it's stealing 45-90 minutes of sleep from you every night. Sleep is your #1 health intervention. Protect it.
**How**: Set an alarm for 9:55 PM labeled "Phone to charger." When it goes off, plug it in the kitchen and walk away. Buy a 50 SEK alarm clock for the bedroom if you use your phone as an alarm.

**That's it for Week 1. Three things. Non-negotiable.**

## 4. The 90-Day Program

### Month 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-4)
*Goal: Build the floor. Establish daily habits. Wake up the body gently.*

#### Week 1 -- Start Moving

| Time | Activity | Duration | Details |
|------|----------|----------|---------|
| 6:00 AM | Morning water + stretch | 7 min | 1 glass water, then the Wake-Up 5 (see below) |
| 12:30 PM | Walk during lunch | 10 min | Walk outside. No phone. Just walk. |
| 7:00 PM | Post-dinner walk | 10 min | Around the block. Take a kid if possible. |
| 10:00 PM | Phone to charger | -- | Kitchen counter. Not bedroom. |

**The Wake-Up 5 (5 minutes, every morning)**
1. Neck rolls -- 10 each direction
2. Cat-cow stretch -- 10 reps (on hands and knees, arch and round your back)
3. Standing hip circles -- 10 each direction
4. Toe touches (or as far as you can reach) -- hold 15 seconds x 2
5. 10 deep breaths -- inhale 4 seconds, hold 4, exhale 6

#### Week 2 -- Add Bodyweight

Everything from Week 1, plus add a 10-minute evening circuit 3 days (Monday, Wednesday, Friday):

**The Starter Circuit (10 minutes)**
Do each exercise, rest 15 seconds between exercises, repeat circuit 2x:
1. Wall pushups -- 10 reps (hands on wall, feet 2 feet back)
2. Bodyweight squats -- 10 reps (sit back like sitting in a chair, go as low as comfortable)
3. Plank -- hold 15 seconds (on forearms, body straight)
4. Standing calf raises -- 15 reps (stand on edge of step if possible)
5. Dead hang or doorframe stretch -- 15 seconds (hang from doorframe or just stretch arms overhead)

#### Week 3 -- Increase Volume

- Morning walk increases to 12 minutes
- Post-dinner walk increases to 12 minutes
- Circuit becomes 3x through instead of 2x
- Pushups move from wall to incline (hands on kitchen counter)
- Plank hold increases to 20 seconds
- Add: 10 bodyweight lunges per leg

**Saturday/Sunday**: One 20-minute family activity (park with kids, walking to the store instead of driving, playing football with the 11-year-old)

#### Week 4 -- Test Yourself

- Morning: 15-minute walk (brisk pace -- you should be slightly out of breath)
- Circuit: 4 days this week (Mon, Tue, Thu, Sat)
- Exercises progress:
  - Counter pushups -- 12 reps
  - Squats -- 15 reps
  - Plank -- 25 seconds
  - Lunges -- 12 per leg
  - Add: Glute bridges -- 12 reps (lie on back, feet flat, lift hips)

**End of Month 1 Checkpoint:**
- You should be walking 20+ minutes daily
- You should complete the circuit without stopping
- Your sleep should be improving
- You should notice slightly more energy in the afternoon

*Goal: Add intensity. Start building real strength. Lock in nutrition habits.*

#### Week 7 -- Push the Cardio

  - Plank -- 40 seconds

**End of Month 2 Checkpoint:**

## 7. Sleep Protocol

Sleep is your #1 health intervention. Not exercise. Not nutrition. **Sleep.** Everything else falls apart without it. A well-rested father with a bad diet beats an exhausted father with a perfect diet every single time.

### The Sleep Rules

1. **Same wake time every day.** 5:50 AM weekdays, 6:30 AM weekends. Non-negotiable. Your circadian rhythm needs consistency.
2. **In bed by 10:30 PM.** That gives you 7-7.5 hours. This is minimum viable sleep for a 45-year-old body under stress.
3. **No caffeine after 2:00 PM.** Caffeine has a half-life of 5-6 hours. A 3 PM coffee is still 50% active at 9 PM.
4. **No screens in bed.** The bed is for sleep and your wife. Not your phone, not your laptop, not "just checking one thing."
5. **Phone charges in the kitchen from 10 PM.** Buy a 50 SEK alarm clock. This one change alone can give you 30-60 more minutes of sleep.

### The Wind-Down Protocol (9:30 PM - 10:30 PM)

```
9:30 PM  -- Dim the lights in the apartment. Switch to warm/low lighting.
9:35 PM  -- Finish any remaining chores or tasks. After this, work is DONE.
9:45 PM  -- Brief connection with wife (10-15 min). Talk about the day.
           No screens during this time.
10:00 PM -- Phone to kitchen charger.
10:05 PM -- Bathroom routine: brush teeth, wash face.
10:10 PM -- Get in bed.
10:15 PM -- Read a book (physical, not tablet) or do 5-minute breathing:
           Inhale 4 seconds, hold 7, exhale 8. Repeat 4 times.
10:30 PM -- Lights out.
```

### Bedroom Environment

- **Temperature**: Keep the room cool (18-19C). Open a window if needed.
- **Darkness**: Use blackout curtains or a sleep mask. Any light disrupts melatonin production.
- **Sound**: If your apartment is noisy, try earplugs or a white noise app (set it on an old phone or speaker, not your main phone).
- **Bed**: Only sleep and intimacy. No working in bed. No eating in bed. Train your brain: bed = sleep.

### Dealing with the 4-Year-Old

If the youngest wakes at night:
- Take turns with your wife. Alternate nights.
- Keep the interaction minimal: low voice, low light, back to bed.
- Don't bring him to your bed unless absolutely necessary -- it resets his sleep habits and yours.
- If he's consistently waking, consider adjusting his bedtime routine (bath at 7 PM, story at 7:30, lights out by 8 PM).

### The Sleep Debt Recovery Plan

You're probably carrying significant sleep debt. You can't "catch up" by sleeping 12 hours on Saturday. Instead:
- Add 15 minutes to your sleep for the first 2 weeks (bed at 10:15 PM instead of 10:30)
- If you can nap: one 20-minute power nap between 1-3 PM on weekends (set alarm -- do NOT nap longer or you'll wreck your night sleep)
- Within 3-4 weeks of consistent 7-hour nights, your sleep debt will largely resolve

## 9. The Daily Minimum

Some days are going to be terrible. The 4-year-old was up at 3 AM screaming. You have back-to-back meetings from 8 to 6. Your ICON deadline is tomorrow. You're exhausted, angry, and the last thing you want to do is exercise.

I get it. Those days will come. Here's what you do on those days.

### The Absolute Minimum (5 Minutes Total)

**This is the floor. You never go below this floor.**

1. **Drink a glass of water.** (30 seconds)
2. **10 squats next to your desk.** Stand up, squat down, stand up. That's it. (1 minute)
3. **30-second plank.** Drop down next to your desk. Hold it. (30 seconds)
4. **Walk to the end of the street and back.** Or one lap around the building. (3 minutes)

**Total: 5 minutes. Zero excuses.**

You will never have a day where you can't find 5 minutes. The CEO of a Fortune 500 company has 5 minutes. A surgeon in a 12-hour shift has 5 minutes. You have 5 minutes.

### Why the Minimum Matters

The point of the Daily Minimum is not fitness. It's identity. Every day you do the minimum, you tell yourself: "I am someone who takes care of his body." That identity is more powerful than any workout program. The habit chain stays intact. Tomorrow you'll feel stronger, and you'll do the full routine.

**The rule: Never have two zero days in a row.** One bad day is human. Two bad days in a row is a pattern. Three is a slide. Don't let the slide start.

### Emergency Energy Boosters

When you're crashing and can't exercise:
- **2 minutes of deep breathing**: Inhale 4 counts, hold 4, exhale 6. Repeat 8 times. Resets your nervous system.
- **Cold water on your face and wrists**: Triggers the dive reflex, instantly increases alertness.
- **10 standing desk stretches**: Neck rolls, shoulder shrugs, hip circles, toe touches, spinal twist. 2 minutes.
- **Step outside for 60 seconds of sunlight**: Natural light resets your circadian rhythm and boosts serotonin.
