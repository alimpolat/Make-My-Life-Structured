## The 12-Week Grip Strength Protocol

Your grip is the weakest link. This runs ALONGSIDE every workout.

### Every Gym Session (Add to End)
1. **Dead Hang**: 3 x max time (aim: 30s → 60s over 12 weeks)
2. **Farmer's Walk**: 3 x 30-40m (start 16 kg/hand → 24+ kg/hand)

### At Home (Daily, 5 min)
- Squeeze a tennis ball: 3 x 20 squeezes per hand
- Or buy a hand gripper (50 SEK on Amazon.se): 3 x 15 per hand
- Towel wringing: wet a towel, wring it dry. Repeat 5x. Old-school but effective.

### Twice Per Week (Add to Pull Day)
- **Plate Pinch**: Hold two weight plates smooth-side-out between thumb and fingers. 3 x 15-20 seconds.
- **Wrist Curls**: Light dumbbell, forearm on knee. 3 x 15 (palm up) + 3 x 15 (palm down).

## Progressive Overload Rules

This is how you get stronger. Follow these rules:

1. **Start lighter than you think.** First 2 weeks, use weights that feel "easy" on the last rep. This prevents injury and builds form.
2. **Add weight when you hit all reps.** If the plan says 3x10 and you complete all 30 reps with good form, add 2.5 kg next session.
3. **If you miss reps, stay at the same weight.** Don't go up until you earn it.
4. **Log every workout.** Use your phone's notes app or a small notebook. Write: exercise, weight, reps. Example: "Bench 12kg x 10, 10, 9"
5. **Never sacrifice form for weight.** A controlled 10 kg is better than a sloppy 20 kg. Especially at 45 — your joints are not 25.

## Safety Rules

1. **If something hurts (sharp pain, not muscle burn), STOP.** Muscle burn is good. Joint pain is bad.
2. **Ask gym staff for form checks.** Friskis staff are there to help. Use them, especially for squat and deadlift form.
3. **Don't ego lift.** Nobody in the gym cares what weight you're using. They care that you're there.
4. **Warm up every time.** Non-negotiable at 45. Cold muscles + heavy weights = injury.
5. **If you miss a week, don't try to "make up" by doubling the next week.** Just restart where you left off.
6. **Blood pressure difference between legs**: Your Perimed appointment is pending. Until cleared, avoid heavy barbell squats with extreme depth. Machine exercises are safer until you get the all-clear.
